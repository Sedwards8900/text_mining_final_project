PHOTOS: Storm damage seen across Central Virginia

by: Kassidy Hammond

Posted: Jun 21, 2023 / 12:33 PM EDT

Updated: Jun 21, 2023 / 10:52 PM EDT

by: Kassidy Hammond

Posted: Jun 21, 2023 / 12:33 PM EDT

Updated: Jun 21, 2023 / 10:52 PM EDT

RICHMOND, Va. (WRIC) — With storms and rainy weather forecasted across Central Virginia for the foreseeable future — or at least for the duration of StormTracker8’s 8-Day Forecast — damage is already being seen across the area as the first official day of summer kicks off on a wet note.

In addition to the storm damage, thousands of Dominion Energy customers, and many traffic lights were left without power Tuesday evening and Wednesday as a result of the storms.

StormTracker8 Forecast

On Cherokee Road near the James River, one large tree limb knocked out power in an entire neighborhood.

Richmond Fire Department Captain Sylvester Henderson urged people in the area to steer clear of any downed power lines, since they can be dangerous when wet.

“Avoid the area. Make the proper notification,” Henderson said. “Treat it as if it’s always energized.”

In Henrico County, Carolyn Nelson said her power went out around 4 p.m. after someone drove into a power line.

She said that recently, her power has been going out frequently due to storms.

“Of all the places I’ve lived in my life — and I’m almost 76 years old — this has been the worst area as far as power going out,” Nelsen said.

As of 11 p.m. on Wednesday, many people living in Central Virginia are still without power.
