Storm knocks out power to tens of thousands across Maine, causes flooding

By Sean Murphy and Associated Press Maine
PUBLISHED 8:37 AM ET May 01, 2023 PUBLISHED 8:37 AM EDT May 01, 2023
SHARE

A storm with heavy rain and gusts knocked out power for tens of thousands of homes and businesses in Maine and washed out some roads as some rivers continued to rise Monday, officials said.

Wind gusts on Sunday reached 70 mph near Matinicus Island, about 20 miles offshore, and 65 mph in Bath, where shipbuilder Bath Iron Works has numerous cranes. There were no immediate reports of serious damage.

Some parts of southern Maine experienced more than 4 inches of rain, and a few topped 5 inches, said Stephen Baron, meteorologist from the National Weather Service.

Central Maine Power, Maineâs largest electricity provider, reported more than 45,000 customers were in the dark as of 8 a.m. Cumberland County had the most outages by far with more than 15,000. By 3 p.m., the number of outages had dropped to just under 15,000 for CMP, with Lincoln County reporting the most outages at just under 4,400.

The National Weather Service states a flash flood warning is in effect until 11 a.m. in areas of Kennebec, Lincoln, Sagadahoc, Waldo, Androscoggin, Somerset, Franklin and Oxford counties. As of noon, the service had extended the warning for the Swift River in Roxbury in Oxford County until "late this evening."

Lewiston, Auburn, Brunswick, Augusta, Waterville, Farmington, Gardiner, Bethel, Turner and Oakland will experience flash flooding, according to the NWS. Flooding forced the town of Gray to close Campbell Shore Road, while a portion of Pequawket Road in Standish was also closed, according to officials in both towns.

Most major school districts remained open as usual, but Regional School Unit 73, which covers Spruce Mountain, announced this morning that it was dismissing all K-12 students at 11 a.m., and pre-K students were being dismissed at 11:30 a.m. The district said on its Facebook page that officials were making the move "out of an abundance of caution."

The outages had dropped to just under 6,000 by 3 p.m. The majority of the outages were in Hancock with just over 1,000.

A flood advisory is in effect until 12:45 p.m. in Hancock, Penobscot and Piscataquis counties because of heavy rainfall and overflowing drainage. 

Gusts up to 50 mph have prompted a wind advisory until 10 a.m. for central and southern Piscataquis, southern Penobscot and interior Hancock counties. 

RIVER FLOODING 

A flood warning continues for areas of the Kennebec, Carrabassett, Pemigewasset, Saco and  Presumpscot rivers in Maine and New Hampshire. 

With reporting from the Associated Press.
