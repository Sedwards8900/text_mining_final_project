West Virginia remains under winter storm warning, much of North Central region to avoid large accumulation

  • by Steven Baublitz STAFF WRITER
  • Dec 16, 2020
  • Dec 16, 2020
  • 0

  • Email

CLARKSBURG, W.Va. (WV News) — West Virginia remained under a winter weather advisory until 4 a.m. Thursday, with the winter storm that’s passing through the area leaving both rain and snow in its wake, as well as icy conditions.

Despite rain prevailing in parts of North Central West Virginia for much of Wednesday, thanks to air that was just slightly above freezing, 2 to 5 inches of snow are still expected in the region, according to National Weather Service Meteorologist Dave Marsalek.

“We still do think there are going to be accumulations in that area,” Marsalek said.

Wednesday night’s mix of rain and snow, he said, could lead to a snow accumulation of 1 to 2 inches by Thursday morning, with a chance of precipitation continuing into the afternoon.

Thursday’s forecast calls for a chance of drizzle and snow before 7 a.m., then a slight chance until 2 p.m., with a chance of snow following. The high will be 35 degrees.

On Thursday night, there is a 50% chance of snow, with accumulation up to 1 inch.

On Friday, the high will be 36 degrees, with a chance of flurries and a slight chance of snow showers.

“It’ll basically be chilly, cloudy, off-and-on snow showers once this main system exits. Not much in terms of accumulation, but you will probably see flakes from time to time,” Marsalek said.

Looking to the weekend, Marsalek said Saturday will be mostly cloudy with a high near 44 degrees, with rain and snow showers likely between 9 p.m. Saturday and 4 a.m. Sunday. The chance of precipitation is 60%

Sunday will see a chance of rain and snow showers before 7 a.m. After that, skies will be mostly cloudy, with a high near 42 degrees. The chance of precipitation is 30%.
