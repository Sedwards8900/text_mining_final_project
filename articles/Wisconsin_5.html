Southeast Wisconsin winter storm brought snow, ice, sleet

By Tom Wachs
Published February 23, 2023
Updated 9:07PM
Weather
FOX6 News Milwaukee

Share

  • Copy Link
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn
  • Reddit

2/23/2023 - A powerful winter storm impacted all of southeastern Wisconsin on Wednesday, Feb. 22. 

Heavy snow occurred in Fond du Lac and Sheboygan counties, stretching into northern Ozaukee county. 

A mixture of snow and sleet pelted areas along the I-94 corridor in Milwaukee, Waukesha and Jefferson counties. 

SIGN UP TODAY: Get daily headlines, breaking news emails from FOX6 News

Meanwhile, significant freezing rain/icing occurred to the south in Walworth, Racine and Kenosha counties. 

Winter storm warnings and ice storm warnings were issued by the National Weather Service for this storm. 

We Energies reported nearly 50,000 people lost power in parts of Walworth, Racine and Kenosha counties as a result of the ice accretion and strong ENE winds.
