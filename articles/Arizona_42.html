Will the 2023 Arizona Monsoon Bring Another Summer of Rain?

Open Audio Article Player

By Staff | on June 30, 2023

Article audio is made possible by CAST11 Prescott Podcast Network. A Talking Glass Media production.

As we roll into July and conditions have become hot and drier, many are likely wondering if the 2023 Arizona Monsoon will bring another summer of rainfall. So what will 2023 bring?

As most already know, Arizona saw a winter with above-average levels of precipitation and well into the year. This is great news as it considerably reduced drought coverage across Arizona. However, with the above-normal winter precipitation and wet soil moisture over the Four Corners, Arizona may see a sluggish start to the Monsoon circulation.

Some Arizona Monsoon Stats

  • Precipitation amounts range from approx 2â³ in the low desert and around 16â³ in higher elevations during Monsoon.
  • Lower areas of Arizona see approximately 10 days of measurable rainfall while higher mountainous areas see closer to 40 days of rain during the Monsoon season.
  • Arizona sees over half a million lightning strikes each Monsoon.
  • Monsoon activity is usually seen in the early to mid-afternoon in higher elevations while lower elevations, usually the Phoenix area, will commonly see activity late afternoon into evening.

2023 Monsoon Outlook

The National Weather Service has predicted that the overall odds for most of Arizona lean toward below-normal precipitation. Western Arizona (Lake Havasu, Kingman, and Yuma) are predicted to have equal chances of a normal, dry, or wet monsoon season.

In addition, all of Arizona should expect to see above-normal temperatures throughout July, August, and September.

Even with lower-than-average odds for Monsoonal moisture this summer, everyone should still remain weather aware and be prepared for Monsoon storms.
