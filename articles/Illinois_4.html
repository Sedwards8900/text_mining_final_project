Central Illinois storms were result of 'perfect set up'

  • BY JIM TAYLOR

    Jim Taylor

    • Author email
  • Jul 3, 2023
  • Jul 3, 2023 Updated Jul 3, 2023

Beneficial rains fell last Thursday in most of the state, but unfortunately that also led to severe weather for areas of central Illinois.

“It was just a perfect set up for it,” said DTN Agriculture Meteorologist John Baranick. “It was really that ridge of high pressure that really brought in those high temperatures, but it was mostly to the south, areas to Texas all the way into southern Missouri so temperatures were well into the triple digits.”

And thunderstorm complexes love to ride the edge of those ridges. Thunderstorms developed over Nebraska and Kansas on Wednesday and held together through Thursday morning in Iowa and Missouri.

“It was then able to tap into more of that heat across Illinois and it just blew up,” Baranick said. “Eighty to 100 mph wind gusts, which flattened corn, ripped up some soybeans and did plenty of structural damage as well.”

The National Weather Service has since confirmed five tornadoes that touched down in central Illinois last Thursday near Chatham, Kincaid, Lincoln, Taylorville and Waynesville. The storms caused over 225,000 power outages in areas around Champaign, Decatur, Springfield and Urbana, according to Ameren Illinois.
