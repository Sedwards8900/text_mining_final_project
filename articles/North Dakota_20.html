Tue. AM UPDATE: High-impact blizzard expected Tuesday into Wednesday across the Northern Plains

By Jacob Morse
Published: Apr. 1, 2023 at 7:44 PM CDT

BISMARCK, N.D. (KFYR) - A Colorado Low will impact the region this week with significant snowfall accumulations, strong winds leading to blizzard conditions, and difficult to impossible travel. (This article was last updated at 5 a.m. Tuesday.)

Snow will begin before sunrise Tuesday along the ND/SD border, pushing north to the I-94 corridor by mid-morning Tuesday, and then overspreading much of the region through the afternoon with heavy snow bands likely.

The initial push of snow Tuesday through Tuesday night will likely be the period of heaviest snow. However, a concerning component of this system is the winds. Winds will be strongest in eastern ND, where gusts could top out at 60 mph (especially in the southeast), whereas 40-55 mph gusts are expected in central ND, and up to 30-45 mph gusts in the west (wind direction will be initially out of the northeast, then the north, and eventually the northwest).

Snow continues on Tuesday night, heavy at times, before tapering off west to east Wednesday with the winds strongest during the day on Wednesday leading to widespread blowing and drifting snow. Stay tuned for forecast updates.

See below for forecast graphics and watch the video above for more information:

Blizzard Warning times by county in North Dakota (CDT):

  • 6 a.m. Tuesday to 12 a.m. Thursday: Burleigh, Morton, Oliver, Foster, Kidder, Stutsman, Grant, Sioux, Emmons, Logan, La Moure, McIntosh, Dickey, Hettinger, Bowman, Adams Counties
  • 6 a.m. Tuesday to 7 a.m. Thursday: Cass, Griggs, Steele, Traill, Barnes, Ransom, Sargent, Richland Counties
  • 12 p.m. Tuesday to 12 a.m. Thursday: Ward, Stark, Renville, Bottineau, McHenry, Dunn, Mercer, McLean, Billings, Slope, Rolette, Pierce, Sheridan, Wells Counties
  • 12 p.m. Tuesday to 7 a.m. Thursday: Grand Forks, Towner, Cavalier, Pembina, Benson, Ramsey, Walsh, Nelson, Eddy Counties

Winter Weather Advisory times by county in North Dakota (CDT):

  • 12 p.m. Tuesday - 12 a.m. Thursday: Burke, Mountrail, McKenzie, Golden Valley Counties

*NOTE THE WIND SPEEDS ON THESE HOUR-BY-HOUR SNAPSHOTS*

**Wind speeds of this magnitude will lead to widespread blizzard conditions and very difficult to impossible travel, especially in central & eastern ND**
