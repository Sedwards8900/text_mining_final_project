    Winter isn't done: Powerful storm will bring snow, rain, could knock out power in RI

    Jack Perry, The Providence Journal
    March 14, 2023 at 9:54 AM·1 min read

    Editors note: For an updated weather report visit our live coverage of the winter storm.

    A powerful coastal storm starting tonight and continuing into Wednesday morning will bring strong wind, rain and snow to the region, according to the National Weather Service.

    With wet, heavy snow expected and the wind gusting up to 60 mph., houses and businesses could lose power.

    The weather service is posting a winter storm warning for the northern part of the state and a winter storm watch for cities and towns in the middle of the state, including Providence, Warwick and East Greenwich.

    "Travel could be very difficult to impossible. The hazardous conditions could impact both the morning and evening commutes on Tuesday," the weather service said in its winter storm warning. "Strong winds could cause tree damage."

    What winter?Providence just had its fourth-warmest winter on record, and there wasn't much snow

    How much snow accumulation is Rhode Island expecting during the nor'easter?

    The storm could drop nearly a foot of snow on northern Rhode Island, 4 to 8 inches to the middle of the state and a few inches along the coast, the weather service says.

    The coast could see pockets of minor to moderate flooding.

    Gale and storm warnings have been posted for the waters around Southern New England. Offshore waves could reach 25 feet.
