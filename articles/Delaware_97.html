    As Tropical Storm Bret becomes hurricane, Delaware will see rain — but not from this storm

    1
    Krys'tal Griffin, Delaware News Journal
    June 20, 2023 at 10:17 AM·2 min read

    Tropical Storm Bret continues to strengthen, but the rainy weather headed for Delaware won't be coming from this storm.

    Tropical Storm Bret, named on Monday, is one of two storm systems moving through record-warm waters between the Caribbean and West Africa, according to the National Hurricane Center.

    Tropical Storm Bret continues moving westward and is expected to become a hurricane in a couple of days – the first of the season – but the storm should not be a cause for concern in Delaware, according to the National Weather Service.

    “Right now, it’s not expected to impact anywhere on the East Coast. It’s supposed to continue moving westward,” said Patrick O’Hara, a meteorologist with the National Weather Service in Mount Holly, New Jersey.

    O’Hara says Tropical Storm Bret will eventually become a low-end hurricane that is expected to diminish over the weekend.

    Where's the forecasted rain coming from?

    Until then, Delaware’s oncoming bout of rainfall and thunderstorms is actually associated with a different weather system, bringing minor coastal flooding and rip currents to coastal areas beginning Wednesday.

    The chances of rain will be high starting Wednesday afternoon and will carry us into quite the rainy weekend, O’Hara said.

    What should Delaware expect?

    The National Weather Service predicts minor coastal flooding for the Delaware beaches and a low probability of widespread hazardous weather in New Castle and Kent counties throughout the next week.

    Gale-force winds and very rough seas are forecast for waters in and adjacent to Delaware beginning on Wednesday.
