    Sunday:

    6:00 p.m. — Over 90% of homes have had their power restored. Just over 20,000 customers and households remain in the dark.

    3:30 p.m. — 85 percent of the 244,000 total customers affected by Friday afternoon’s storms have had power restored, according to Evergy.

     Another round of scattered storms pass through the greater Kansas City area Sunday evening

    As crews wrap up work in cities surrounding Kansas City, like Topeka and Lawrence, they will make their way to the metro to assist in the recovery effort ahead of the next wave of storms.

    9:00 a.m. —

    As of 9 a.m. Sunday, over 85 percent of customers affected by Friday afternoon’s storms have had power restored.

    Roughly 25,000 households remain in the dark.

    According to Evergy, this storm response is now the largest mobilization of crews in the power company's history and is still growing. Nearly 2,000 Evergy employees and 1,000 responders from neighboring states have responded to assist in the recovery effort.

    Nearly 500 power poles were damaged or completely destroyed across Evergy's service area. Many of the remaining outages will require tree removal and reconstruction.

    Outages in Topeka and Lawrence should be resolved by Sunday night.

    Statement from Evergy:

    By 10 p.m. Saturday, 75 percent of customers affected by the Friday afternoon thunderstorms across our service area have had power restored.

    All available Evergy and contract line crews continue to work around the clock to restore power and will do so until power is restored to all customers. Personnel from neighboring utilities in Iowa, Wisconsin, Illinois, Nebraska and Oklahoma are in the field with more help continuing to arrive from other areas. Sunday morning, we will have the largest staging area for line crews and storm restoration the area has experienced as most of our assisting crews will be onsite.

    About 35,000 customers are without power as of 10 p.m. Saturday. We expect outages in the Topeka and Lawrence areas to be restored by Sunday night. Outages impacting small groups of customers in the Kansas City metro area are expected to continue into early next week.
