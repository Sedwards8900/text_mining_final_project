Winter storm most likely to develop into bomb cyclone, forecasters say

  • Updated: Mar. 13, 2023, 5:35 p.m.|
  • Published: Mar. 13, 2023, 11:59 a.m.
By
  • Tristan Smith | tsmith@masslive.com

The strong high-impact winter storm that’s sweeping across New England and bringing up to 18 inches of snow to parts of Massachusetts, has a strong likelihood of developing into a bomb cyclone.

A late-winter Nor’easter is forecasted to bring heavy wind and rain throughout the majority of New England Monday evening and develop into a strong winter snowstorm all of Tuesday into Wednesday morning.

  • Read more: Here’s when rain is expected to change to snow in Massachusetts

“The storm has the strong potential to turn into a bomb cyclone,” said National Weather Service Boston Morning Coordination Meteorologist Glenn Field.

The weather forecaster said all signs are pointing toward the Nor’easeter, named Winter Storm Sage, to develop into a bomb cyclone when it hits its peak Tuesday.

  • Read more: What does the weather term bomb cyclone mean, and is it coming to Massachusetts?

A “bomb cyclone” is a technical term used to define a weather storm that has a central pressure drop by at least 24 millibars in 24 hours or less. Basically, the rapidly dropping pressure creates high-intensity winds and a potentially damaging storm, according to forecasters.

Weather Channel forecasters also believe the storm has a strong chance of forming into a bomb cyclone near the eastern coast late Monday night.

  • Read more: Mass. weather: ‘High impact’ storm with high winds, snow beginning Monday

Western, central and eastern Massachusetts have been placed under a winter storm watch that’s expected to bring up to 18 inches in certain areas and high winds creeping up to 60 mph. The warning is issued to go into effect Monday at 8 p.m. and continue on until Wednesday at 8 a.m.

Residents are warned that “travel could be very difficult to impossible.” Hazardous conditions could impact both commutes on Tuesday, so forecasters urge taking an extra flashlight, water and food in your vehicle.

  • Read more: Winter storm coming to Mass.: 5 maps and everything you need to know

The Massachusetts coast will be under a high wind warning from 8 a.m. Tuesday to 8 a.m. Wednesday. Winds could reach as high as 30 to 40 mph, potentially as high as 60 mph. Forecasters warn of difficult travel for high-profile vehicles, blown trees and power outages are all possible, with peak winds expected Tuesday afternoon into the evening.
