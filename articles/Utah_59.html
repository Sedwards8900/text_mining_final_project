Another winter storm? Yup, and it’s heading for the Southern Utah mountains

Written by or for St. George News
March 9, 2023
Livestock feed on hay during a snowstorm in Cedar City on Jan. 25, 2023 | Photo courtesy of Robert Allen, Cedar City News/St. George News

ST. GEORGE — Winter just doesn’t seem to want to go away in Southern Utah, where again, the five-county area of Washington, Iron, Beaver, Kane and Garfield will be under a winter storm warning this weekend.

Between 11 a.m. Friday and 6 a.m. Sunday, heavy snow is possible above 8,000 feet, with total accumulations expected to be 10-20 inches with locally higher amounts, according to the National Weather Service. Winds could gust up to 55 mph.

A strong Pacific storm has moved ashore, bringing with it “heavy rain with flooding, significant snow and strong winds,” the NWS said on its website.

Brian Head could see the most significant snowfall from the storm, which is expected to move in overnight Thursday-Friday and result in patchy blowing snow with south-southwest winds of 29-32 mph. Winds will increase Friday with 3-5 inches of snow predicted during the day and another 3-5 inches by Saturday afternoon, the weather service said.

Snow will begin to taper off overnight Saturday-Sunday, with 1-3 inches of additional accumulation expected at higher elevations and temperatures ranging from the mid-20s to the mid-30s.

Residents in Milford and Beaver won’t see much in the way of snow, but wind is another story. Sustained winds are expected to reach 26-36 mph Friday afternoon and evening and continue into Saturday, with gusts approaching 50 mph. There also is a 70% chance of rain.

St. George will see more springlike weather through the weekend, with lows in the 40s and highs in the 60s with light winds and a 50% chance of rain and/or thunderstorms.
