N.J. weather: Nasty weekend storms to hit state with 3 inches of rain, 50 mph wind gusts

  • Updated: Apr. 28, 2023, 9:28 a.m.|
  • Published: Apr. 28, 2023, 7:45 a.m.
243
shares
By
  • Jeff Goldman | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com

A powerful duo of nasty storms packing wind gusts up to 50 mph and heavy downpours that could total 3 inches of rain are in the forecast to bookend the last weekend of April.

While there will be a dry period for part of Saturday, expect damp, dreary conditions starting today through at least Sunday night, forecasters say.

The first of the two systems will start bringing rain to New Jersey by this afternoon and be accompanied by 15 to 20 mph winds that gust 25 to 35 mph gusts this afternoon and as high as 40 to 45 mph by tonight, the National Weather Service said.

Heavy rain is expected Friday night and could continue through the overnight. Isolated thunderstorms also can’t be ruled out in South Jersey.

Conditions improve during the day Saturday with rain tapering off from south to north and winds diminishing to about 10 to 15 mph, the weather service said in its Friday morning forecast discussion.

In all, the weather service says most of the state will get about an 1 to 1.5 inches of rain from the first storm, with locally higher amounts of 2 inches possible along the Jersey Shore, where the heaviest of the rain sets up.

AccuWeather.com is less optimistic about a lengthier stretch of rain-free conditions on Saturday.

“The duration of the dry weather may only be limited to a few hours during Saturday afternoon or evening,” AccuWeather said.

High temperatures on Friday will be in the upper 50s to around 60. Saturday’s highs will widely vary with northwest areas only reaching the low 50s and part of South Jersey climbing into the mid-60s.

The second, more severe storm arrives Sunday with light rain possible in the morning and then heavier precipitation from mid-day to evening likely to bring an additional 1 to 1.5 inches of rain, the weather service said.

Northwestern areas will get more rain, with lower amounts southeast of the Interstate 95 corridor. Gusty winds are also in the forecast for Sunday evening and night as temps climb into the 60s.

The weather service’s New York office, which covers Bergen, Essex, Hudson, Passaic and Union counties, says the second storm will deliver three-quarters of an inch to an inch of rain.

Minor flooding will be limited to high tides along the Jersey Shore because the storm will move through fairly quickly, according to the weather service.

We will dry out on Monday — the first day of May — with sunny skies, highs climbing into the low 60s and breezy conditions.
