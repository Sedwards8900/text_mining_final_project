Missouri Tornado: 5 Killed In Latest Severe Early-Season Storm, Officials Say

Brian Bushard
Forbes Staff
I cover breaking news for Forbes
Following
Apr 5, 2023,02:18pm EDT|

Topline

The death toll from a catastrophic tornado that ripped through southern Missouri early Wednesday morning has climbed to five people, local officials said Wednesday afternoon, as yet another deadly tornado rips through the country.

Key Facts

The tornado, which touched down at roughly 3:30 a.m. and carved a rural area in Bollinger County, Missouri, south of St. Louis, caused widespread damage, including uprooted trees and power lines, and destroyed homes and businesses.

The Bollinger County Sheriffs office said in a Facebook post cited by the Associated Press five people have died, after the Missouri State Highway Patrol, which has been assisting in local recovery efforts, initially confirmed injuries and fatalities from the storm.

The tornado ripped through an area roughly 15-20 miles long, staying on the ground for about 15 minutes, National Weather Service meteorologist Justin Gibbs told the Associated Press.

Tangent

The tornado in Missouri is the latest in a series of early-season tornadoes so far this year, following a major one last week in Little Rock, Arkansas, injuring at least 600 people and leaving behind extensive damage. Last month, a series of tornadoes in Mississippi killed 26 people and destroyed hundreds of homes and businesses. A January tornado south of Houston caused widespread damage.

Surprising Fact

Another tornado that ripped through Delaware Saturday was the widest in state history, according to the NWS. That tornado, which was one of the states two strongest, carved a 14-mile path in its wake and killed one person.

Big Number

422. Thats how many tornadoes the National Weather Service has recorded so far this year, including a whopping 194 last month. That compares to 357 tornadoes in the first three months of last year, 208 in 2021 and 242 in 2020. The states with the most tornadoes so far this year are Alabama (77), Illinois (49), Georgia (42) and Mississippi (34).

What To Watch For

More tornadoes. Tornado watches are in effect in eastern Arkansas and Missouri, as well as western Kentucky, Ohio and Tennessee, as well as southern Illinois and Indiana, according to the National Weather Service. Forecasters warn the Ohio River Valley is at an enhanced risk of severe thunderstorms through Wednesday.
