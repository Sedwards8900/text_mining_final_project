Winter Storm Watch: Heavy snow for all of Michigan’s biggest cities

  • Updated: Mar. 02, 2023, 1:04 p.m.|
  • Published: Mar. 02, 2023, 5:13 a.m.
630
shares
By
  • Mark Torregrossa | mtorregr@mlive.com

UPDATE: Most hazardous times to be on the road in Friday’s storm

A winter storm watch has been issued for the entire southern half of Lower Michigan. The winter storm watch is alerting you of a high probability of heavy snow coming.

The winter storm watch goes into effect at noon Friday for all of the far southern counties and at 3:00 p.m. Friday for the rest of southern Lower from the entire Grand Rapids region to the Saginaw Valley region. The entire winter storm watch area goes through Friday evening and ends at 2:00 a.m. Saturday. This snowstorm will be a 12 hour moderate-to-heavy snow at any one spot in the southern half of Michigan.

The winter storm watch has a northern boundary from Muskegon to the Big Rapids area, Mount Pleasant area, Midland County and Bay County. All of the big cities of the southern half of Lower Michigan are in the winter storm watch.

The heavy snow swath will fall in the winter storm watch area. Most of the winter storm watch area can expect 6 inches of snow to as much as 10 inches of snow. The exception will be far southeast Lower Michigan, including the Detroit area, Monroe and maybe Ann Arbor. That southeast corner will have some occasional freezing rain and rain mix with the snow and lower snow amounts to 4 inches to 6 inches.

After two ice storms in the past two weeks we are weary of freezing rain. This storm does not look like it will have a significant ice accumulation in the places with freezing rain. I would call it a light glaze possible in the Detroit area, Monroe and possibly Ann Arbor.

Here is the radar forecast from noon Friday to 8:00 a.m. Saturday.

It’s pretty easy to see from the radar forecast the southern half of Lower Michigan will all be in moderate to heavy snow Friday afternoon and Friday evening.

Here’s a good place to start with a snowfall forecast map.

The forecast above is the European Model’s ensemble forecast. This forecast shows the most likely snowfall amounts. It won’t show any heavier streaks with an inch or two more of snow. It also probably shows the snow a little too heavy in the mixed precipitation area of far southeast Lower Michigan.

Late today we will be able to use the high resolution weather models to see if any small areas of even heavier snow are expected.

For now it’s a pretty simple snowstorm story. Snow starts early Friday afternoon and continues to very early Saturday morning. The first half of the snowstorm will have wet snow. After dark Friday evening the snow will become fluffy and drift on 35 mph wind gusts.

It could be a bright and beautiful Saturday morning with some sun and a fresh thick blanket of snow.
