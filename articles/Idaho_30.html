Lewiston Police, City Crews Hard at Work After Friday Afternoon Storm Causes Flooding on Many Local Area Roads

  • Jaymin Stamper | Big Country News Connection
  • Jun 9, 2023
  • Jun 9, 2023 Updated Jun 9, 2023

LEWISTON, ID - A heavy storm that moved through the Lewiston area Friday afternoon has made quite a mess of local area roads. 

Many roadways are experiencing standing water or have been flooded due to the amount of precipitation that came with the storm. Staff with the Lewiston Police Department and City of Lewiston Public Works Department are actively making their way around town working to address the many affected areas.

"We want to assure you that our dedicated LPD staff and the City's Public Works Department are actively addressing these localized flooding issues," read a press release from the City of Lewiston issued Friday afternoon. "Lewiston Emergency Management is also actively assessing the situation to determine the necessary resources and actions required to effectively address and mitigate flooding."

As of 3:30 p.m. Friday afternoon, the flooding had caused the closure of Southway Avenue (from 8th to Snake River Ave) and Gun Club Road (from Nez Perce Dr to Stewart Ave) in Lewiston, ID. Many other roads are also being affected including Bryden Canyon Road, Bryden Avenue, Idaho Street, Thain Road and Grelle Avenue. 

The storm and flooding has also caused multiple power outages in Lewiston affecting approximately 1,250 Avista customers.

The National Weather Service has issued a flood warning for Nez Perce and Asotin counties that will remain in effect until Friday evening. 

"In the interest of your safety, we strongly advise everyone to minimize travel and avoid driving on the roads during rainstorms like the one we experienced this afternoon."

If you come across any property damage affecting public safety, please report it by calling the non-emergency phone line at 208-746-0171. For life-threatening emergencies, call 911.
