    Delaware County, all of southeastern Pennsylvania in T-storm watch

    Share this:

    • Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)
    • Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)
    By Daily Times |
    July 25, 2023 at 1:00 p.m.

    Thunderstorms are popping up all over eastern Pennsylvania and a severe thunderstorm watch is in effect Tuesday until 8 p.m. for Delaware County and the entire tri-state region.

    “Instances of flooding are possible, especially in urban and poor-drainage areas and near vulnerable small creeks and streams,” reads the alert from the National Weather Service office in Mount Holly, New Jersey.

    At 12:30 p.m., storms were crossing Berks, Lehigh and northern Montgomery counties with a thunderstorm warning there as well. The northern tip of the Chesapeake Bay was also in a warning.

    Daytime heating had not yet begun to kick in before the storms arrived, with temperatures most places in the tri-state region in the low-to-mid-80s. The clouds might well break later in the day and evaporate the moisture, cranking up the humidity.

    The AccuWeather forecast is for partly sunny and hot Wednesday with a high near 90 degrees, with the heat building Thursday through Saturday with highs in the mid-90s and an occasional threat of storms.

    Those sustained conditions will be the hottest of the summer so far, if they occur.
