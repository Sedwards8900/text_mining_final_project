First Alert Overnight Weather Forecast: July 26

RALEIGH, N.C. (WTVD) -- First Alert: A heat wave (3 or more days at 95+) is expected for Central NC today through Saturday. Feels-like temps will top out at 102-110 each afternoon through the weekend.

There may be a few more clouds approaching from the southeast later this afternoon as a sea breeze front sparks some widely-separated thunderstorms, but there's only a very slight chance that they can push as far inland as the Triangle before they fall apart early this evening. Otherwise, we'll have just a few clouds tonight.

The flow switches more to the southwest starting Thursday, knocking back the extent of sea-breeze storms a bit and allowing a clearer path to breaking new ground for this summer as we approach the century mark.

The rest of this week is going to feature the Bermuda high stretching westward to hold hands with the prominent upper ridge in the Southwest,

resulting in an extremely large high pressure ridge under which we will remain through at least early Saturday.

Friday & Saturday are the hottest days of the stretch as we get to 98, but with AccuWeather RealFeel Temperatures peaking over 105 each day and even low temperatures holding over 75 each night through Saturday, relief will be hard to come by until the hot and humid pattern changes.

That change will come on Saturday in the form of an arriving shortwave that beats back the ridge, with a thunderstorm signaling a break in the dry pattern.

Those lowering heights as the upper trough sets in will allow highs to drop into "just" the lower 90s on Sunday. Highs will then settle closer to 90 for the rest of the forecast period.

Have a great evening!
